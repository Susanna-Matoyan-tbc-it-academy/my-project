Psychology & Body Language

I've always been fascinated by psychology — how people think, feel, and
communicate beyond words. Body language, in particular, is incredibly
interesting to me: a look, a gesture, or posture can say more than many
sentences. Studying these nonverbal signals helps me understand others
and myself better.

What draws me to psychology

Growing up, I noticed how small signs and reactions reveal true
emotions. Psychology books taught me frameworks to interpret
behaviour, while real-life observations made the lessons meaningful.
Reading about motivation, habits, and communication gave me tools to
improve my relationships and study habits.

Body language: small signals, big meaning

Body language is a silent language we all use. Eye contact, hand
movements, and posture can show confidence, nervousness, interest, or
boredom. Learning to read these cues helps in conversations,
interviews, and teamwork. I enjoy practicing this awareness and
applying it in everyday life.

Short reflection: Psychology helps me be more empathetic and curious
about people — and that's a big part of who I am.
